Phone Mouse Support & Privacy

Welcome to the support and privacy page for Phone Mouse. Below you'll find relevant information regarding how to get help, as well as how we handle your data responsibly.

Getting Started

Setup is simple:

  1. Install Phone Mouse on both your iPhone and Mac
  2. Make sure both devices are on the same WiFi network
  3. Open the app on your Mac first (it will appear in your menu bar)
  4. Open the app on your iPhone
  5. Tap your Mac's name to connect
  6. Start moving your cursor!

Two Control Modes

Gyro Mode: Move your phone in the air like a magic wand to control the cursor. Best for presentations and pointing from across the room (hold phone upright).

Trackpad Mode: Use your phone's screen as a wireless trackpad. Best for precise control and close-range use (hold phone horizontally).

Common Issues

Can't see my Mac in the iPhone app?

  • Ensure both devices are on the same WiFi network
  • Check that the Mac app is running (look for the mouse icon in your menu bar)
  • Try closing and reopening both apps
  • Restart your WiFi router if needed

Connection keeps dropping?

  • Move closer to your WiFi router
  • Check that your Mac isn't going to sleep
  • Ensure "Low Power Mode" is off on your iPhone

System Requirements

iPhone/iPad: iOS 18.0 or later, iPhone 6s or newer

Mac: macOS 15.0 or later, Apple Silicon or Intel processor

How Phone Mouse Works

Phone Mouse operates entirely on your local network:

  • Your iPhone and Mac connect directly to each other
  • All communication happens between your devices only
  • No data ever leaves your local network
  • No internet connection is required (after download)

Permissions

Phone Mouse requests the following permissions:

Motion & Orientation: To use your phone's gyroscope for cursor control in Gyro Mode

Local Network Access: To connect your iPhone to your Mac over WiFi or Bluetooth

These permissions are used solely for the app's functionality. No data from these sensors is ever stored or transmitted outside your devices.
